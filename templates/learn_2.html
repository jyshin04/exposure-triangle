Aperture is the opening in the lens that lets light into the camera.

It's measured in f-numbers like f/1.8, f/4, or f/16.

  • Lower f-number (e.g., f/1.8) = wider opening = more light
  • Higher f-number (e.g., f/16) = smaller opening = less light

Think of it like the pupil of your eye. In the dark, your pupil gets bigger to let in more light.
