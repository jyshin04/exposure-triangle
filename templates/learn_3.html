Aperture Affects Background Blur

Aperture doesn’t just change brightness — it also affects depth of field, or how much of the image is in focus.

  • A wide aperture (e.g., f/2.8) creates a blurry background and a sharp subject — great for portraits.
  • A narrow aperture (e.g., f/16) keeps more of the scene in focus — useful for landscapes.

Tip!

Want blurry backgrounds? Use a lower f-number.

Want everything sharp? Use a higher f-number.
